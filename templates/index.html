CLLM Review

Reviewer Instructions

You are reviewing claims and evaluations that were automatically extracted from research papers by an LLM. Your task is to assess the quality and accuracy of the LLM's output by comparing it against the original paper text.

Workflow

  1. Select a paper below to begin reviewing.
  2. Enter your name when prompted — this is used to save and load your review.
  3. For each result, read the grouped claims and the LLM's evaluation, then indicate whether you agree or disagree.
  4. For each claim, expand the claims list and accept or oppose individual claims. Use "View in paper" to see the original source text.
  5. Add comments where needed to explain your reasoning.
  6. Click "Mark Complete" when you have reviewed all results.

Your progress is automatically saved as you review. You can hit "Back" to return to the paper list or close the browser entirely — just enter the same name to pick up where you left off. If you'd like to start over, choose "Start new review" when prompted.

Run Metrics

Hover over the Run column in the papers table to see the model, cost, and processing time for the extract and eval pipeline stages.

Key Definitions

SUPPORTED
Claims are adequately supported by evidence in the paper.
UNSUPPORTED
Claims lack sufficient evidence or are contradicted by the paper.
MAJOR
A core finding central to the paper's conclusions.
MINOR
A background claim, secondary observation, or methodological detail.

Review Actions

Agree / Disagree
Whether you concur with the LLM's evaluation of a result. Disagreeing reveals override dropdowns.
Accept / Oppose
Whether an individual claim was correctly extracted and accurately stated.
Flag
Mark a result for further discussion, independent of agreement.
View in paper
Opens a side panel with the source passage highlighted.
